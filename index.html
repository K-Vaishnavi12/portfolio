About

Calm systems-thinker. Slightly nerdy. Mildly obsessed with shipping.

I fell into AI three years ago through a half-working chatbot and never really left. What started as curiosity turned into a real engineering practice — building agents that don't just generate text, but do things: call tools, hold state, make decisions, and clean up after themselves.

Most of my work sits at the messy seam between research and product — LangGraph orchestration, retrieval pipelines, multilingual interfaces, and the unglamorous deployment work that turns a notebook into something a stranger can use on a Tuesday morning.

I care most about AI that serves people LLMs usually ignore. India has 1.4 billion people; most models still treat that as a rounding error. My current project ships in Telugu first, English second.

Right now: building Bazaar Buddy v2, picking up system design & RAG, grinding Java DSA, and prepping for 2027 placements.

  1. 01
    2025 / in progress / ★ featured

    Bazaar Buddy

    Voice-first agentic AI shopping assistant — built Telugu-first, English second.

    • 1.4 billion Indians speak a language LLMs are still bad at. Bazaar Buddy starts with one of them.
    • A grandmother sends a Telugu voice note — "I need something for my grandson's birthday, under five hundred rupees" — and the agent figures out the rest.
    • A LangGraph state machine decodes intent, occasion, and budget, then routes through product-catalog tools and replies in her language, by voice.
    • Designed so the next billion users aren't an afterthought — they're the default.
    • Currently in active development; v2 is teaching it to remember conversations.
